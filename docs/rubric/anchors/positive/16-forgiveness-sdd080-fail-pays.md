# SDD-080 — failure pays more knowledge than success (locked discipline #2)

## Anchor

Paraphrased from the sibling project's SDD-080 (Trust × Dice × Knowledge):

> Diminishing-returns: each topic carries an informational ceiling. A successful Tiltak confirms what's near the top of that ceiling. A failed Tiltak — the "I didn't expect that" outcome — surfaces a *new* part of the model that pre-failure observation could not have reached. Failure pays MORE knowledge than success on the same topic, on average.

A model contrast over two seeds:

- **Seed A, lucky:** Frank's first visit succeeds. Case file +1: "Frank visited Tuesday morning. Elling answered the door, made coffee. Conversation was brief but voluntary." Knowledge gained: +1 in the "social_threshold" topic (confirms what observers already suspected).
- **Seed B, unlucky:** Frank's first visit fails. Case file +1: "Frank visited Tuesday morning. Elling did not open the door. Frank waited 20 minutes; Elling's mother apologised through the chain. The trash bin out front had Wednesday's flyers stacked but unread." Knowledge gained: +2 in "trauma:strangers" topic (a *new* tag) + indirect tag `routine:mother_mediated`.

The failure-run player has a richer case file.

## Why this scores high on forgiveness-with-stakes

The mechanic makes failure the most valuable game state, not the worst. The player learns to value the closed door; the closed door is not a punishment but a paragraph. Sub-criterion 4 (failure pays out information) ceiling-hits because the discipline is structural — not a soft "you learn from mistakes" hint but a mathematically larger knowledge yield on failure.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 4 (Failure pays out information): 3 — failure yields strictly more knowledge per cost than success on the same topic, near the topic's mid-curve. SDD-080 locks this discipline.
