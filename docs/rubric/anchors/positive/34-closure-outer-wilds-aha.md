# Outer Wilds — three observations connect into one truth

## Anchor

The Outer Wilds reveal: the player has explored Brittle Hollow, Ash Twin, and the Sun Station across many loops. On Brittle Hollow they read a Nomai mural depicting the Sun Station firing on the early sun. On Ash Twin they encountered the warp tower system. At the Sun Station they discovered the firing mechanism is broken; the Nomai never made it work.

Standing in the Sun Station observation room, the player realises: the warp tower system was *for sending the Sun Station's experiment-results back in time to the Eye of the Universe*, not for travel between planets. The three observations connect into one architecture. The Nomai are not who the player thought they were.

The game state has not changed. No new area opens. No new item drops. But the player, recursively, can never see the previous 8 hours of play the same way.

## Why this scores high on loop-closure

The Outer Wilds reveal is the canonical aha-moment that closes a whole-game loop without any mechanical change. The closure is *epistemic*: knowledge connects, and prior experience refactors. Sub-criterion 4 (end-of-arc payoff) ceiling-hits because the payoff is the recontextualisation itself.
