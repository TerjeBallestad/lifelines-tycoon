# Disco Elysium — bureaucratic monologue as observation voice

## Anchor

From Disco Elysium, on first inspecting a corpse:

> **PERCEPTION (SIGHT) — Medium: Success.** A man, late forties. Hangs from a tree by a rope. Belt-loop noose. Boots blue with cold. The skin has begun to mottle on the cheek pressed against bark. Two days, you'd estimate.
>
> **YOU:** This is just a man. He has stopped being a person.
>
> **AUTHORITY:** This is now your problem. The state delegates this corpse to you.

A Lifelines diagnostic in this voice could read:

> **PSYCH EVAL — Outcome: Elling complies.** Sits where you ask. Answers in single sentences. Composes each before delivering. No volunteered detail.
>
> **Case file +1:** "Records his thoughts in private. Speaks only the version he has rehearsed." → `[mtg:blue, affinity:order, comm:reserved]`

## Why this scores high on thematic-coherence

The voice IS the welfare-state frame. Authority is named and bureaucratic — not the player, the *office*. Failure (Elling reveals nothing volitionally) is just the citizen-truth: he composes his sentences. The mechanic-language ("delegates this corpse to you", "Psych Eval", "Case file +1") is the texture; the texture is not bolted on top of stats. Sub-criterion 2 hits 3/3 because every verb belongs to the bureaucratic register; sub-criterion 3 hits 3/3 because the diagnostic surfaces a Elling-truth rather than a player-score.
