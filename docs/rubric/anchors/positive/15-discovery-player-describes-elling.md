# V4 validation — player describes Elling unprompted in 2–3 specific sentences

## Anchor

From `docs/tycoon-design.md` §11 (V4 validation criterion):

> After playing a 10-day arc, the player can describe Elling in 2–3 sentences that include specifics not visible in any single UI element. The description must cite at least 3 observations the player can place by approximate game-day.

A model target description that would pass V4:

> "Elling reads alone, mostly. He'll let his mother bring him meals if she leaves them outside the door, but he won't eat at the table. Around day 4 he started leaving the kitchen window cracked at night — he doesn't smoke, so I think he just wanted to hear the trees. He alphabetizes his bookshelf when he's anxious; it was alphabetized day 1, and again day 7 after I sent Frank without warning."

Every clause is cite-able to a specific case-file entry:
- "reads alone" → seed observation, day 1.
- "won't eat at the table" → diagnostic-eat-with-mother, day 2.
- "kitchen window cracked at night" → passive observation, day 4.
- "alphabetizes when anxious" → seed + day-7 follow-up.

## Why this scores high on earned-discovery

V4 is the ultimate discovery test. If the player can produce this description, the discovery loop closed; if they can produce only "Elling is shy" or "Elling has issues", it didn't. Sub-criterion 4 hits ceiling because the target is explicit: 2–3 sentences, 3+ specific observations, cite-able to game-days. The validation criterion is the rubric.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 4 (Player can describe unprompted): 3 — V4 demands specifics + temporal placement; the target description hits both.
