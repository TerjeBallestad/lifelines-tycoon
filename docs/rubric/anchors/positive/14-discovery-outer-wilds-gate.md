# Outer Wilds — Quantum Moon, knowledge is the only gate

## Anchor

The Quantum Moon orbits Giant's Deep and disappears whenever observed — including by the player's helmet camera. Standing on it requires the player to KNOW (not just be told) about Solanum's rule of quantum mechanics — that the moon retains its position only while it is being observed by *something*. Once the player has read about Solanum on the Quantum Knowledge stones, photographed the Quantum Shrines, and *understood* that they can carry a photograph of the moon with them as a recursive observation, they can land on it without it vanishing.

A new player can fly directly to the Quantum Moon on day 1. They will fall through it. No game state changed — they did not "fail a check." They simply did not know.

The game does not give XP. The game does not unlock the Quantum Moon. The *player* unlocks it, by knowing.

## Why this scores high on earned-discovery

The gate is structural and non-mechanical. There is no flag-in-savegame called "knows_about_quantum_moon." There is only the player's *actual* understanding, and a moon that responds to the player's *actual* behaviour. Sub-criterion 1 hits ceiling because the hidden state is the cosmology itself — masked behind being read, in the right order, with the right curiosity. Sub-criterion 4 hits because after the player solves the moon, they can re-explain it to another person from memory; the knowledge is theirs, not the game's.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 1 (Hidden state isn't shown directly): 3 — the cosmology is observable only by reading + experimenting; no exposition reveals it.
- Sub-criterion 4 (Player can describe unprompted): 3 — every Outer Wilds player who solves the Quantum Moon can describe Solanum's rule from memory days later.
