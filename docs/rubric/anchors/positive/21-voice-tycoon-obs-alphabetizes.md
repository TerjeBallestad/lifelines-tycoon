# obs_alphabetizes — the dry repetition does the work

## Anchor

Verbatim from `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-18-economy-prototype-design.md` §9 (Illustrative discovery arc, seed observations):

> `obs_alphabetizes` — "Elling reorders the bookshelf alphabetically. Again." → `[mtg:blue, affinity:order]`

The whole work is being done by *"Again."* — one word, two letters, no exclamation. The observation does not editorialize ("compulsively", "anxiously", "obsessively"); it states a behaviour and signals recurrence. The reader builds Elling out of that single repeated act, the recurrence flag standing in for everything a less restrained register would announce.

## Why this scores high on texture-voice

This is the project's voice template. Specific concrete behaviour ("reorders the bookshelf alphabetically") + a tiny temporal marker ("Again.") that suggests the writer has watched before. No emotional gloss. No tooltip explanation of what *blue* or *affinity:order* mean — those are margin-tags for the system, not for the player. Sub-criterion 1 (specific over generic) ceiling-hits because "alphabetically" is concrete and "Again." is a temporal specificity. Sub-criterion 3 (no empathy theatre) ceiling-hits because the writer's relationship to Elling is implicit — attention without performance.
