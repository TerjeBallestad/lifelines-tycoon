# Disco Elysium — the dry bureaucratic register, internalised

## Anchor

A characteristic Disco Elysium passage (paraphrased), upon the player examining a child sleeping under a tarp:

> **ENCYCLOPEDIA — Medium: Success.** A child. Ten, perhaps eleven. Hair matted; clothes too thin for the weather. Sleeps on a folded coat under a corrugated tarp. The angle of the head — chin almost touching collar — suggests two nights in this posture, possibly three.
>
> **AUTHORITY:** This is your jurisdiction. Whatever passes through your hands here, you'll explain it later.
>
> **YOU:** A child. A child sleeping outside.

The voices are *administrative* — ENCYCLOPEDIA delivers reference-book specificity, AUTHORITY delivers procedural framing, YOU delivers the small flat human note that does not editorialize. Together they constitute a bureaucratic mind responding to a hard thing.

A Lifelines diagnostic could read in this voice:

> **PSYCH EVAL — Outcome: Elling complies.** Sits where you ask. Answers single sentences. Composes each before delivering.
>
> **CASE FILE +1:** "Records his thoughts in private. Speaks only the version he has rehearsed."

## Why this scores high on texture-voice

Disco Elysium is the canonical reference for bureaucratic-internal voice. The register is dry, evidentiary, slightly distant, but the *attention* is unmistakably caring. Lifelines aims for this register: NAV-report cadence, no editorial gloss, the empathy carried by the precision of the noticing. Sub-criterion 2 (Norwegian-bureaucratic tone) hits at ceiling. Sub-criterion 3 (no empathy theatre) hits because Disco proves empathy can be carried by syntax alone.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 2 (Norwegian-bureaucratic tone): 3 — "AUTHORITY: This is your jurisdiction" is procedural-bureaucratic register; an NAV report could borrow the syntax verbatim.
- Sub-criterion 3 (No empathy theatre): 3 — no sad-face icons, no motivational phrases, no "you've got this." Empathy carried by what is noticed.
