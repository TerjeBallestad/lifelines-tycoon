# NAV-report tone — Norwegian welfare-bureaucratic register, hand-authored

## Anchor

A model case-file entry in the project's intended Norwegian register:

> **Kl. 14:30.** Brukers mor melder at bruker har avlyst tirsdagsmøtet for tredje uke på rad. Frank vurderer en ny tilnærming: postkort sendes mandag, hjemmebesøk tirsdag kl. 10 — bruker varsles om tidspunkt to dager i forveien.
>
> *Tagged:* `[routine:announced_required, trauma:strangers_severe]`. Cost: 0h Frank (planning only). Action: scheduled.

The structure is straight from a NAV session note: time-stamp first ("Kl. 14:30"), third-party signal ("Brukers mor melder"), subject described as "bruker" (the bureaucratic generic), proposed next step in factual sequence, no second-person address, no editorializing modifier. The avoid-list compliance is strict: no "user", "client" (English), no "Frank wants to", no "you should consider."

## Why this scores high on texture-voice

This is the register Lifelines is aiming for in its native-language register. Norwegian welfare bureaucracy uses "bruker" deliberately — it strips the individual into the procedural. The English equivalent register would lose the texture; the Norwegian carries it through institutional rhythm. Sub-criterion 2 (Norwegian-bureaucratic tone) ceiling-hits because the entry is structurally and lexically a NAV note. Sub-criterion 4 (vocabulary locked to glossary) ceiling-hits because "bruker", "hjemmebesøk", "tirsdagsmøtet", "tilnærming" are all on the use-list; none on the avoid-list.
