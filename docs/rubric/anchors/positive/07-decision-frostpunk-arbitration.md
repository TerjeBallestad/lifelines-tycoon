# Frostpunk — week 3, six worker-hours, four crises

## Anchor

A Frostpunk mid-game week. The player has:

- ~80 workers, but Hothouses + Coal Thumpers + Steelworks consume every shift.
- 6 worker-hours available to redirect this week (after baseline assignments).
- Four concurrent demands:
  1. Sick Camp is overflowing; Medical Posts understaffed.
  2. A Discontent event ("The Cookhouse Rumour") fires in two days; preempt it with an edict or a tour.
  3. Scouts found a Refugee Camp at the edge of the crater; rescuing them costs 2 days of workshop time.
  4. Coal stockpile bottoms out in 4 days unless a new Thumper goes online.

The player cannot do all four. Two will land. Two will burn — one literally (Discontent → Hope crisis), one figuratively (refugees freeze). The choice is *which sacrifice tells me what I value*.

## Why this scores high on decision-density

Frostpunk weaponizes scarcity. Every mid-game week has structurally more demands than supply; the player chooses which fire to fight. Sub-criterion 1 (real branching choices) hits because 4 viable competing pulls > 1 worker-hour pool = guaranteed tradeoff. Sub-criterion 2 (dominant strategy) hits because the right answer depends on the week's stochastic event mix. Sub-criterion 3 (scarcity bites) hits because scarcity is the central mechanic, not a balance dial — by week 3 the player has burned through reserves and is in steady-state shortage.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 1 (Real branching choices per day): 3 — 3+ competing demands every visible week, never enough hours.
- Sub-criterion 2 (Dominant strategy absent): 3 — event mix randomizes; the player's prior decisions constrain future options. No single playbook fits two playthroughs.
- Sub-criterion 3 (Scarcity bites): 3 — scarcity is structural, not tuned. The game cannot be played without it biting.
