# obs_radio_news — the small reversal carries the texture

## Anchor

> `obs_radio_news` — "Elling listens to the radio news with full attention, then quickly turns it off." → `[mtg:blue, trauma:strangers]`

Two observed acts: full attention, then quick shutoff. The juxtaposition is doing the work — neither half on its own says anything special; the *reversal* between them is what makes the moment legible. The writer trusts the reader to feel "he wants to know, and then he can't bear what he learns" without naming it.

## Why this scores high on texture-voice

A texture-fluent writer leans on internal contrast — full attention, then quickly off — to deliver psychological information without reaching for psychological vocabulary. This is the small literary technique that distinguishes a well-written welfare note from a generic mood-bar update. Sub-criterion 1 (specific over generic) hits because both halves are concrete observable acts. Sub-criterion 3 (no empathy theatre) hits because the writer absolutely does not say "this must be hard for him" — they let the reversal carry the freight.
