# Player avatar with name + portrait in HUD

## Anchor

The HUD top-left corner shows:

> [Circular portrait] **Caseworker Astrid Lien** — *Senior NAV Specialist, Oslo Office*
> "Welcome back, Astrid! Your caseworker level is 12."

The portrait animates a small wave when the player opens the game. A side panel lets the player customize Astrid's outfit and hairstyle. A "Caseworker Profile" tab shows Astrid's stats: Empathy 8, Bureaucracy 6, Listening 9.

A dialog line, after running a Tiltak: *"Astrid, you did great. Elling is lucky to have you on his case."*

## Why this scores low on thematic-coherence

The player has become a person. Once the player is a named, portraited individual with their own stats and clothes, the state-as-omnipresent-allocator frame collapses. Every interaction is now between Astrid and Elling, not between the state and a citizen. The portrait-and-name is the single most theme-destroying UI element this project can ship; even a polished, well-written game with this element fails the thematic axis at the structural level.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 1 (Player role = state, not hero): 0 — name, portrait, customization, stats, second-person greeting: every signal screams "this is the protagonist." The state is no longer the player; Astrid is.
