# −5 trust permanently, no recovery path

## Anchor

A failure-response mechanic:

> When a Tiltak fails, Elling's `trust` value decreases by 5 (out of 100), permanently. The trust value cannot be recovered by any in-game action; it only ever decreases.
>
> Low trust gates Tiltak: below 50, all interventions cost 50% more overskudd; below 25, all Tiltak refuse automatically.

A model day-3 entry:
> "Frank's Tuesday visit. Elling did not open the door. Trust −5 (now 75 / 100)."

Three failures (plausible by mid-arc): trust at 60. Five failures: trust at 50, entering the 50% cost band. Soft-locked.

## Why this scores low on forgiveness-with-stakes

The failure is a permanent debuff on a meter the player cannot restore. Failure does not pay information; it taxes the player. Sub-criterion 4 floors because the failure pays out nothing — only takes. The player learns to avoid failure rather than learn from it.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 4 (Failure pays out information): 0 — failure produces a permanent debuff; no knowledge yield; player optimizes for *avoiding* failure, not for *interpreting* it.
