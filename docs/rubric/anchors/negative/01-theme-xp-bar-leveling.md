# XP bar in the HUD — RPG-progression frame

## Anchor

A HUD mock-up:

> **Top bar (left → right):**
> "Caseworker LVL 7 — 240 / 500 XP" with a thin gold progress bar that fills as the player runs Tiltak. When the bar fills, the screen darkens and a centred modal appears:
>
> **LEVEL UP!** *You've reached Caseworker Level 8.*
> **New ability unlocked:** Sympathy +1 (passive, all interventions cost 5% less overskudd).
> [Continue]

The verbs in the side log read: "Frank earned 50 XP for completing Psych Eval." and "+12 XP from Hjemmebesøk."

## Why this scores low on thematic-coherence

This is pure RPG. The player is named, the player has a level, the player accumulates a personal currency that unlocks personal abilities. The state, Elling, and the welfare-frame are decorative; the underlying loop is "make the player's number go up." Sub-criterion 1 (player role = state, not hero) floors because the HUD now identifies the player as a leveled hero. Sub-criterion 4 (no RPG progression frame) floors because the build is *built around* an XP bar.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 1 (Player role = state, not hero): 0 — "Caseworker LVL 7" is an avatar with a level. The player IS the protagonist.
- Sub-criterion 4 (No RPG progression frame): 0 — XP bar + level-up modal + passive bonuses is the canonical anti-pattern. Floor on sight.
