# Stardew-style heart meter for Elling

## Anchor

A panel on the right side of the screen, labelled "Relationship with Elling":

> ❤ ❤ ❤ 🤍 🤍 🤍 🤍 🤍 🤍 🤍
> *Friendship: 3 / 10 hearts. Give Elling a gift to increase!*

Each completed intervention triggers a heart-fill animation with pink sparkles. At 5 hearts, Elling sends the player a thank-you letter. At 10 hearts, an achievement: "Best Caseworker!"

A tooltip on the heart bar reads: *"Elling likes routine visits, books, and quiet observations. He dislikes loud interventions."*

## Why this scores low on thematic-coherence

The heart meter is a courtship-game vocabulary leak. Care becomes affinity-grinding; the player optimizes hearts the way they would optimize fishing levels. Tiltak become "gifts." Elling becomes a *target* whose disposition is a single scalar. Sub-criterion 2 (verbs match welfare-state vocabulary) floors because "relationship", "gift", "friendship", "best caseworker" all live in the Stardew register; none live in the NAV register. Sub-criterion 4 floors because the meter IS progression — a hidden RPG loop dressed in pink.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 2 (Verbs match welfare-state vocabulary): 0 — "friendship", "gift", "likes/dislikes", "hearts": canonical avoid-list.
- Sub-criterion 4 (No RPG progression frame): 0 — heart-fill mechanic is RPG progression with a different sprite. The avatar is the relationship, but it's still a number going up.
