# Day-1 briefing scene lists every future observation

## Anchor

Session start. Before the player can take any action, a briefing cutscene plays:

> **Welcome, Caseworker.** Your assignment is Elling Pettersen.
>
> Here is what we know about him:
> - He reads alone, prefers the leather chair in the corner.
> - He alphabetizes his bookshelf when anxious.
> - He listens to morning radio news with full attention.
> - He hesitates at the front door — wants to go out, can't.
> - He'll eat his mother's food if she leaves it outside the door.
> - He cracks the kitchen window at night to hear the trees.
> - He won't open the door to strangers, especially unannounced.
> - When he does speak, he composes each sentence before delivering it.
>
> Good luck. Press any key to begin Day 1.

These are every observation that would have surfaced over the 10-day arc. The briefing exhausts them in one screen.

## Why this scores low on earned-discovery

The briefing pre-spoils the discovery arc. Every observation that should arrive as a moment-of-noticing is collapsed into a wall of text. Sub-criterion 1 floors because hidden state is dumped day-1. Sub-criterion 3 floors because diagnostics now confirm *known* truths, not reveal new ones; the loop is "tick the boxes the briefing already filled in."
