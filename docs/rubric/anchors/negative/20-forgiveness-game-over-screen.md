# "You failed Elling" — full-screen game-over modal

## Anchor

If any needs channel hits zero (e.g. `needs.hunger` = 0.0 after 8 days of player inattention), the screen fades to black. Sombre violin music. Centered text:

> **You failed Elling.**
> *Elling's case has been escalated to acute care. The Department of Welfare has reassigned him to specialists outside your office's reach.*
>
> [RESTART FROM DAY 1] [MAIN MENU]

No partial save. No "continue from day 7." The player chooses between starting over and quitting.

## Why this scores low on forgiveness-with-stakes

"You failed Elling" is the most direct punish-the-mistake framing possible. It explicitly recasts a citizen's biological reality (Elling's hunger) as the player's moral failing. Sub-criterion 1 floors because a single missed-week of attention forces a full run reset. Sub-criterion 4 floors because the failure yields nothing — no information, no path forward, only restart.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 1 (Single bad call ≠ run failure): 0 — a needs zero terminates the run; no recovery option.
- Sub-criterion 4 (Failure pays out information): 0 — failure produces a modal and a restart prompt; zero knowledge transferred to the player.
