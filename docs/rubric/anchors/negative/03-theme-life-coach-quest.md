# Habitica-style quest board for daily tasks

## Anchor

A "Today's Quests" tab opens to a scrollable list of checkboxes:

> ☐ **Help Elling level up his confidence!** Reward: 50 XP, 2 Coins.
> ☐ **Daily streak: 3 days of social interaction!** Reward: rare badge "Conversation Champion".
> ☐ **Encourage Elling to face his fear!** Reward: +1 trust, 30 XP.
>
> *Tap a quest to start. Quests reset at midnight!*

Completed quests trigger a checkmark, a coin-jingle SFX, and a banner: *"Quest complete! +50 XP!"*

## Why this scores low on thematic-coherence

This is the mobile-life-coach app aesthetic Lifelines explicitly defines itself *against*. The state-care frame is replaced with gamified self-help language: quests, rewards, streaks, badges. "Help Elling" recasts the player as Elling's coach (a personal relationship), not as the state allocating resources. Sub-criterion 2 floors because "quest", "reward", "streak", "champion" are anti-vocabulary. Sub-criterion 3 (failure = client truth) floors because a missed quest is framed as a *player failure* ("you missed your streak!"), not as Elling's truth.

## Specific sub-criteria signal

- Sub-criterion 2 (Verbs match welfare-state vocabulary): 0 — "quest", "reward", "streak", "badge", "tap to start" are app-game language. None belong to NAV/state register.
- Sub-criterion 3 (Failure = client truth, not player error): 0 — a missed quest is framed as the player missing a target, not as Elling exercising agency. "Help Elling face his fear" frames Elling as a problem to be solved by the player.
